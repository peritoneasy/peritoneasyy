Impact

The dialysis market is projected to reach $209.75B globally by 2034. PeritonEasy reduces treatment-related complications, improves patient quality of life, and aligns with CMS incentives for home dialysis. By filling the gap in customizable dialysate, it offers a scalable and impactful innovation in kidney care.

Manufacturing

PeritonEasy was designed with manufacturing feasibility in mind to keep per-unit costs low at scale. Early estimates indicate a competitive per-unit cost when produced at volume; detailed cost breakdowns and supplier lists are maintained internally and available to collaborators under NDA.

Regulatory Pathway

PeritonEasy is expected to be classified as a Class II device under FDA guidelines, following the 510(k) pathway. Predicate devices include Baxter’s HomeChoice Pro Automated PD System. The device will comply with relevant standards (21 CFR 876.5630, ISO 10993, IEC 60601-1, ISO 11607, FDA SaMD guidance, ISO 14971). Sterility and electronic safeguards will be incorporated into manufacturing. Intellectual property includes modular refillable cartridges, physician-adjustable algorithms, and ultrasonic sensor validation loops specific to PD.

Patents

The team has reviewed related technologies and is developing an IP strategy focused on protecting the novel aspects of PeritonEasy (cartridge modularity, clinician-guided formulation workflows, and validation loops). Specific patent filings and detailed prior-art analysis are being managed with advisors and are not published here.

Reimbursement

CMS incentivizes home dialysis via the Advancing American Kidney Health initiative. ESRD patients qualify for Medicare regardless of age. PeritonEasy integrates seamlessly into current workflows and is expected to qualify under existing billing for PD supplies and services. Aligns with CMS cost-containment and value-based care models, supporting broad reimbursement through Medicare and Medicaid.
